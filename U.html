Understanding the Basics

What is Agriculture?

Agriculture is the science, art, and practice of cultivating soil, growing crops, and raising livestock to produce food, fiber, and other goods that sustain human life.

In simple words — it is the process of growing plants and rearing animals for food and raw materials. It began nearly 12,000 years ago when early humans learned to plant seeds instead of only gathering wild food, marking the start of settled civilization.

Role of Agriculture in Human Life

  • Provides food, nutrition, and daily sustenance
  • Supplies raw materials to industries like textiles and food processing
  • Creates employment for a large share of the world's population
  • Supports rural economies and community livelihoods
  • Shapes culture, tradition, and the rhythm of rural life

Growing Food, Growing the Future

Agriculture feeds nearly eight billion people, anchors economies from village markets to global trade, and shapes the land, water and air we all share. Every meal on every table begins in a field — tended by a farmer who works with the seasons, not against them.

As we look ahead, the choices we make about how we farm will decide the health of our soil, the security of our food, and the wellbeing of the millions who grow it. Let us farm a little more gently, waste a little less, and thank a farmer a little more often.

"To forget how to dig the earth and to tend the soil is to forget ourselves."
